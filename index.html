Who I Am

Bridging IP Doctrine
with Living Practice

I am an LLM candidate in Intellectual Property Law at Queen Mary University of London (Centre for Commercial Law Studies), with a background in law and commerce. My work is grounded in the UK and EU IP frameworks, with a particular focus on how those regimes are evolving in response to digital commerce and emerging technology.

As a paralegal at Carlton Elleker Chase in London, I manage trademark prosecution and enforcement matters across WIPO, EUIPO, and UKIPO frameworks, draft letters before claim and witness statements for contentious IP disputes, and advise on copyright, royalty cases, and brand regulatory agreements involving globally recognised clients.

Prior to this, I contributed to IP valuation reports for Fortune 100 clients at OxFirst, an IP valuation consultancy in London, supporting patent infringement proceedings and licensing rate determinations used in live legal matters.

I am also Co-Founder and COO of Lexdoo, an AI legal platform now serving 1,500+ users and 15 law firms across three continents — where I led all IP and commercial legal structuring. This sits at the intersection of two areas I care deeply about: intellectual property law and the future of legal technology.
